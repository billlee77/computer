Graduate Students:

Funding is available for several students. Depending on your interests and goals, you would have the opportunity to contribute to both projects, or concentrate your efforts on one.

Proton structure studies in Mainz, Germany. Proton spin polarizabilities are fundamental observables of proton structure, and are sensitive to the internal dynamics of the internal QCD constituents that make up the proton's spin. Our experiment will utilize both polarized beams and target in order to extract all four of the proton's spin polarizabilities for the first time. Students would gain experience working in an international collaboration, yet the facility is small enough for the student to be exposed to nearly every aspect of the experiment. Research would include data analysis and simulations performed in Canada, and laboratory work in Germany. For more details see the research page.

         

Cerenkov Detector Development. This detector will be used for pion/kaon separation in Hall C at Jefferson Lab. Research would include simulations, detector prototyping, and testing. The student may also have the opportunity to contribute to other hardware and software projects in Hall C.


How to Apply: Applicants should have a first class academic standing, intense motivation, and solid reference letters attesting to a strong ability to do research at the undergraduate level. Preference will be given to applicants with relevant prior experience in nuclear or particle physics research. Foreign applicants are typically expected to write the TOEFL or IELTS English proficiency exams. The relevant application forms can be found here.
